# Asset Group

The `Asset Group` module is used to organize assets into categories or groups. This makes asset management easier and keeps similar assets structured under a common group.

## Asset Group List

![Asset Group List](/img/basedata/asset-management/asset-group/asset-group-list.png)

Go to `Asset Management > Asset Group` to view the full list of asset groups.

### Information Shown on This Page

- **Active**: Shows whether the group is currently active.
- **ID**: System-generated group ID.
- **Name**: Name of the asset group.
- **Created On**: Date when the group was created.
- **Actions**: Options to edit or delete the group.

### Top Controls

- **Search**: Quickly find a group by name or ID.
- **New**: Opens the form to create a new asset group.
- **All**: Displays all groups.
- **Active**: Displays only active groups.
- **Inactive**: Displays only inactive groups.

## Add an Asset Group

![Asset Group Add](/img/basedata/asset-management/asset-group/asset-group-add.png)

Follow these steps to create a new asset group:

1. Go to `Asset Management > Asset Group`.
2. Click the **New** button.
3. Turn the **Active** toggle on or off based on your requirement.
4. Enter the group name in the **Asset Group Name** field.
5. Click **Submit** to save the group.

### Field Description

- **Active**: Enable this if the group should remain available for use.
- **Asset Group Name**: Enter the name of the asset group. This field is required.

## Edit an Asset Group

![Asset Group Edit](/img/basedata/asset-management/asset-group/asset-group-edit.png)

Follow these steps to update an existing asset group:

1. Find the group from the list.
2. Click the **Edit** icon in the relevant row.
3. Update the group name or active status as needed.
4. Click **Submit** to save the changes.

## Delete an Asset Group

![Asset Group Delete](/img/basedata/asset-management/asset-group/asset-group-delete.png)

Follow these steps to delete an asset group:

1. Locate the group in the list.
2. Click the **Delete** icon.
3. In the warning popup, click **Delete**.
4. Click **Cancel** if you do not want to proceed.

:::warning
If a group may still be needed in the future, consider marking it inactive instead of deleting it.
:::
